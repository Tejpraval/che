Summary

The availability of water in India varies significantly by region and is influenced by factors such as rainfall, surface water sources, and groundwater reserves. States with higher annual rainfall and abundant river networks tend to have greater water availability, while those with arid or semi-arid climates often face water scarcity. Detailed percentages of water availability for each state can be obtained from official reports from the Central Water Commission or the Ministry of Jal Shakti, which provide comprehensive statistics and analysis. Addressing issues related to water availability requires a combination of sustainable management practices, efficient use of water resources, and ongoing monitoring and conservation efforts.
